History of Lychee

Lychee is a tropical fruit native to southern China, with a history of cultivation dating back over 2,000 years. It was a favorite fruit of Chinese emperors and has spread to many parts of the world.

What is Lychee?

Lychee is a sweet, fragrant tropical fruit native to southern China. It has juicy white flesh inside a rough red skin.

Fun Fact #1

Lychee has been cultivated for over 2,000 years and was a favorite of Chinese emperors!

Fun Fact #2

Lychee is rich in vitamin C and antioxidants that support your immune system.

Where Do They Come From?

Litchi chinensis is native to Southern China, but its subspecies are spread across Asia:

  • Chinensis: China, Vietnam, Thailand
  • Philippinensis: Philippines
  • Javensis: Indonesia, Malaysia

Litchi chinensis chinensis

This is the main cultivated subspecies, widely grown in China and Southeast Asia. Known for large fruit and excellent sweetness.

Litchi chinensis philippinensis

Native to the Philippines, this wild lychee has smaller fruit and is less commercially cultivated, but important for genetic diversity.

Litchi chinensis javensis

Found in Indonesia, this rare subspecies is adapted to tropical lowlands and is less studied but holds unique traits.

How to Grow a Lychee Tree from Seed

1. Choose a Ripe Lychee

Pick a large, reddish-purple lychee fruit. Remove and save the big brown seed inside.

2. Soak the Seed

Rinse and soak the seed in warm water for 3 days, replacing the water daily. Plant it once the seed starts to crack.

3. Planting the Seed

Use a container with drainage holes and slightly acidic soil. Bury the seed about an inch deep.

4. Watering & Sunlight

Water the plant every other day. Keep it in a warm, shaded place until leaves appear. Then move it to indirect sunlight.

5. Growth & Patience

Lychee trees can take 5 to 20 years to bear fruit. Prune regularly and repot yearly as it grows taller.
